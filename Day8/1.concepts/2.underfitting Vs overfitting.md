# Overfitting vs Underfitting

| Aspect | Overfitting | Underfitting |
|--------|-------------|--------------|
| **Definition** | The model learns the training data too well, including noise and unnecessary details. | The model fails to learn the underlying patterns in the data. |
| **Training Performance** | Very high | Low |
| **Testing Performance** | Low | Low |
| **Generalization** | Poor; performs poorly on unseen data. | Poor; performs poorly on both training and unseen data. |
| **Model Complexity** | Usually too complex | Usually too simple |
| **Learning Behavior** | Memorizes the training data instead of learning general patterns. | Does not learn enough from the training data. |
| **Bias** | Low Bias | High Bias |
| **Variance** | High Variance | Low Variance |
| **Common Causes** | Too many features, insufficient training data, model trained for too long, overly complex model. | Too few features, overly simple model, insufficient training, important information missing. |
| **Effect** | Excellent performance on training data but poor performance on new data. | Poor performance on both training and testing data. |
| **How to Reduce** | Simplify the model, use regularization, collect more data, use cross-validation, apply feature selection, or use early stopping. | Increase model complexity, add relevant features, improve feature engineering, or train the model longer (if applicable). |

## Example

| Scenario | Training Accuracy | Testing Accuracy | Interpretation |
|----------|------------------:|-----------------:|----------------|
| Good Fit | 95% | 93% | The model generalizes well. |
| Overfitting | 99% | 80% | The model memorizes the training data. |
| Underfitting | 68% | 65% | The model has not learned the underlying patterns. |
